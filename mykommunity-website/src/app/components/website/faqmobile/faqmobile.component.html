Technology

Why is Biometric not an ideal solution?

While biometric data provides a secure way to identify residents, it is highly sensitive and needs to be dealt with utmost care. Also, the infrastructure requirements are very high and IT Act 20 requires that all biometric data be renewed every 15 days.

Why is Access Card not an ideal solution?

People tend to forget access cards or lose them, and thus can be misused by anyone who has the card. Plus, printing cards and the churn rate is very high.

Why is RFID not an ideal solution?

Even when residents vacate their flats they can still retain the apartment RFID. This creates an issue. Additionally, infrastructure to set up RFID tags, RFID readers is quite extensive. Not just that, the infrastructure cost is also financially intensive.

e-Intercom

What is & e-Intercom?

It is a mobile-based intercom which is used for automating visitor validations. This feature will give you an authorization phone call to a number you have previously set (either mobile or landline), if you’re unable to complete the in-app notifications to approve or deny a visitor’s (Guest / Delivery Executives / etc.) entry to the society.

How can I set my e-Intercom number?

You can set a mobile or landline number as your e-Intercom number from the app. There can be only one e-Intercom number per flat/home. This e-intercom call gets triggered only when the resident does not respond to the app notification. By default, the primary e-Intercom is set to the mobile number of the first person who downloads the app for the flat. Residents who have not downloaded the app can also have an e-Intercom number. For this, they have to request the admin to set a fixed landline or mobile as the e-Intercom of the flat.

How to stop calls from MyKommunity?

MyKommunity calls you for visitor validation on your mobile. If you don’t want to receive this phone call you can remove your mobile number from e-Intercom and put the mobile number of someone who can approve on your behalf. If you want, you can leave the e-Intercom numbers blank too. To reset the e-intercom number, go to Side menu -> open e-Intercom and then edit the numbers.

I am going out of the country for travel. How can I stop calls from MyKommunity?

In this case, you can remove your mobile number from e-intercom and put the mobile number of someone who can approve on your behalf. If you want, you can leave the e-intercom numbers blank too. To reset the e-intercom number, go to Side menu -> open e-Intercom and then edit the numbers.

I am getting a notification for a visitor (e.g. cab, delivery executive) that I did not expect. What should I do?

This is likely due to an error in flat selection by the guard at the gate. You can inform the guard about this and ask him to be more attentive when taking entries at the gate.

I am at work and don’t want to be disturbed for visitor approval. My family should be able to do it. What should I do?

    You need to
  1. Switch off app notifications in the app
  2. Assign an e-intercom number where the approval calls should go. If other members (s) of your family have also downloaded the app, then they will receive an in-app notification to authorize the entry. If no response is received from in-app notifications, calls will be initiated in the order of priority, first to the primary number and then to the secondary number.

Is it compulsory to set an e-Intercom number?

No, it’s not compulsory to set an e-intercom number. You can leave the e-intercom number blank. However, we recommend that you set at least the primary number so that you don’t miss visitor validation calls from the security.

How does the authentication of surprise guests, cabs, courier, delivery executives etc. work?

For every visitor at the gate without having a pre-approved OTP, we initiate an authorization process with the residents of a flat. It is important to understand how we recognize numbers/users in our system first. Registered users – Residents must be registered in the system for MyKommunity to communicate with them. Owners and tenants can register themselves by sharing their details via their facility office. Further, they can register their family easily using ‘Add family’ feature in the app. Active users – When registered users download the app, they become active users. Active users can designate one mobile/landline number as an e-intercom number. This number is given priority when system calls are generated for approval.

What is ‘Advance’, ‘Dues’, ‘Overdues’ on the Payment page?

Advance – The amount you pay over and above the invoice amount is the advance. Dues – The date by when your payment is supposed to be paid. Overdues – Bills that are not paid on or before the due date are overdue. Let’s say yesterday was the last day to pay the bill but the payment was not cleared then for today, the invoice becomes overdue.

How do I make an Advance Payment?

Click on “Community” tab on the app screen Click on “Payments” Click on the arrow mark next to Advance/ Dues/ Overdue at the right corner Select “Pay Advance” Enter the amount you want to make as an advance payment Click on “Pay Advance” Select the Payment Type via which you want to make the payment and “Pay”

What happens if you make payment of an amount more than your due amount?

The difference gets added to the advance. When the new invoice is generated, the bill amount gets settled from advanced.

What is the difference between the invoice date, due date and overdue? / Where can I find the invoice date, due date?

Invoice date – The date on which invoice is raised by the society management team. You can find invoice date beside the status of every invoice. Due date – The date on or before which the payment has to be made else a fine/penalty fee might be charged. Due date is mentioned at the bottom of every invoice. Overdue – If the invoice cross the due date then the invoice becomes overdue. If an invoice is not cleared on or before the due date, the status changes from ‘Due’ (blue colour) to ‘Overdue’ (red colour).

What is Financial Year?

In India, gated communities are commonly following financial year based auditing & tax filing. The financial year starts from 1st April of the current year and ends on 31st March of next year.

I cannot see the ‘Pay Now’ button to make payment. Why?

Online transactions are allowed in the current financial year only. The selected financial year in the app may be incorrect. Please change the financial year from the drop down option under “Payments” menu and you will find the “Pay Now” option.

How can I see my payment receipts of the previous financial years?

What is the meaning of each filter in the Payments section?

New – New bill/Invoice Overdue – Bills/Invoice that has crossed the due date Paid – receipt of the payments you have made Pending – Bill/Invoice that “I Have Paid” intimation raised, but not accepted/ rejected by the society management team Rejected – Bill/Invoice that “I Have Paid” intimation raised and rejected by the society management team.

Should I use ‘Pay dues’ or ‘Pay advance’ to make payment?

It is advised to make the payment by selecting “Pay dues”. You can use the “Pay advance” option in case you do not have any dues to pay. This will decrease the chances of the late fee being charged incorrectly.

‘Dues’ is showing some items with the name “From previous year”. What is this?

When one financial year ends, the outstanding dues of the previous year get carry forwarded to the new financial year. The carry forwarded items will have the tag “From previous year”, it is the unpaid dues of the previous financial year.

Why Convenience fee is charged?

Here we are using Payment Gateways instead of direct transfer like NEFT/RTGS/P2P UPI. There is a Convenience fee for online payments in Housing Societies which payment gateways either charge to customer or association. Normal merchants like Online shopping platform will bear the charges themselves, the case of Residents Association is different. Hence the user is being charged.

Helpdesk

What is Helpdesk?

It is a complaint management system to ensure a quick and convenient way to track complaints via MyKommunity app. When you face a society related issue(for eg. Electrical, plumbing), you can raise a complaint through the MyKommunity app. The complaint will be forwarded to the Society Management or Facility Manager. Further, he/she will update the complaint and assign it to the respective society staffs. You will be notified about the assigned staff and ETA and the action taken against your complaint. You can also add comments, attach photo/documents and reopen a closed complaint.

How to raise a service request?

  • Click on “Community” tab on the app screen
  • Click on “Helpdesk”
  • Click on “Raise Complaint” present at the bottom of the app screen
  • You can select the category and type of issue, select “Personal” if the issue is related to your house and “Community” if the issue is related to the common area of your society.
  • Provide details related to the issue
  • You can attach up to 3 supporting documents and photos
Note: In case you want to further communicate with the society management you can further add comments and documents/photos.

What is the process flow for the service desk/helpdesk complaints raised? How does the escalation matrix work?

The moment residents raise a complaint/query (service request) using the helpdesk feature in the MyKommunity app, the service request will be sent to the “Helpdesk Manager” and resident will also receive the SMS/email notification with service request ID and subject.

If the “Helpdesk Manager” does not take actions within the stipulated time, then the service request will get escalated to the “Facility Manager/Manager”.

Despite, still no actions taken, then the service request will get escalated to the “Admin” who will be the last point of contact.

When the admin/Facility Manager, updates the status or add any comment to the complaint, the resident will again receive the email which will have the service request ID in the subject line.

Residents could go to “Helpdesk” menu under the “Community” tab and click on the specific service request/complaint and view the changes made by the admin/Facility Manager.

The app will be updated with the progress/action taken by the Admin/Facility Manager against the complaint.

Can a closed complaint be reopened?

Yes, Resident can reopen a closed complaint any time.
  • Go to the “Helpdesk” menu under “Community” Tab.
  • Click on the specific complaint which is “Resolved”
  • Click on “Reopen” and enter the comments and “Re-open” the resolved/closed complaint.

How to filter the Complaints according to the category or status of the complaint?

  • Select a specific category from the dropdown option.

    • To filter according to the status:

  • Go to the “Helpdesk” menu under “Community” Tab.
  • Click on “Status” present on the top of the app screen.
  • Select a status (New, In Progress, Closed) from the dropdown option.
